Use of Images

I used the Chrome inspect to determine the format of the images Stack overflow used on their website.

Images can come in two main forms; raster images or vector images. Raster images are usually more detailed and are larger files while vector images are generally less detailed and can be scalable. This means that a vector image can change size to fit to a screen. (Unitec MMWD PPT Image)

Stack overflow's images are Compressed Scalable Vector Graphics Images (.svgz). This was a good design choice as vector images are quite simple and using a more complex raster image may have created an unnessecarily large image file which would have slowed down the loading time of the page.

Stack overflow also shows good design in where it has used images. The images on the blog tell users about what is happening in the corresponding articles - they are not there just as eye candy.
